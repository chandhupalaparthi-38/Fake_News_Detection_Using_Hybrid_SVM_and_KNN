Fake News Detection using Hybrid SVM and KNN
The project aims to develop a hybrid model for fake news detection on social media, merging Support Vector Machines (SVM) and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN). It emphasizes understanding complex dynamics among users, news content, and social networks, integrating social capital in the framework. The model incorporates multiple machine learning classifiers for enhanced detection. The study focuses primarily on Twitter for analyzing fake news spread, with future research aimed at considering a wider range of social media platforms. The proposed model's effectiveness in detecting fake news is evaluated using Confusion Matrix and ROC Curve analyses, showcasing its robustness and potential for combating misinformation on social media.

Abstract

The proliferation of fake news on social media has become a critical issue, necessitating innovative detection technologies. Traditional methods relying solely on linguistic and compositional analysis often prove insufficient against evolving misinformation tactics. To address this challenge, a groundbreaking hybrid model has been introduced, combining Support Vector Machines (SVM) and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) to effectively detect fake news. This model considers the intricate dynamics between users, news content, and social networks within the framework of social capital, prioritizing key factors that influence fake news detection. Incorporating various machine learning classifiers ensures robustness and generalizability, enhancing fake news detection systems against sophisticated tactics.
